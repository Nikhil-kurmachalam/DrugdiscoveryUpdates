What do High / Medium / Low mean?
🟢 High — Major clinical or regulatory milestone: Phase 3 results, FDA approval/decision, breakthrough therapy designation, or a landmark mechanism-of-action paper. Act-on-it news.
🟠 Medium — Meaningful progress worth tracking: Phase 1/2 data, new preclinical target validation, notable biomarker findings, or resistance/off-target signals. Monitor closely.
⚪ Low — Background signal: early-stage research, methods papers, industry reports, or general pipeline updates without specific data readouts. Good for awareness.

With an API key, Claude classifies each article. Without one, keyword heuristics are used.

What do the Stages mean?
🎯 Target ID 🔬 Preclinical 🏥 Clinical ✅ Approval ⚙️ Method 🏭 Industry

Stages are applied to all sources (news, preprints, and PubMed) by the AI classifier or keyword heuristics — not just PubMed. Every article is assigned a stage based on where it falls in the drug discovery pipeline.
